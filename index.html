Section 02 — Reflective Journal

PPW Journal

Five reflective entries on what I learned through the Professional and Personal Writing module at SLIIT.

01

Professional Communication Skills

Before PPW, I assumed technical skills alone determined career success. The module fundamentally changed this — I learned that communication is a core professional competency. IT professionals must write reports, emails, proposals and documentation. I practised professional emails, structured reports, and formal letters, and learned how to calibrate tone depending on the audience.

Key Takeaway Communication is as technical as coding — it requires precision, structure, and audience awareness.
02

Career Planning & Self-Awareness

The Career Development Planning exercise required genuine self-assessment. I mapped my strengths (React, Spring Boot, Node.js, Java), weaknesses (limited leadership, time management under pressure), opportunities (growing tech industry, remote work), and threats (rapid technology change). This SWOT exercise gave me a structured view of my professional landscape and a clear career direction.

Key Takeaway I now have a concrete roadmap: Full-Stack Engineer → Senior Developer → Software Architect.
03

CV Writing & Personal Branding

PPW taught me a CV is a personal marketing tool, not just a list of facts. I learned to tailor it to specific roles, quantify achievements, and structure content strategically. More importantly, I understood personal branding — my GitHub profile, LinkedIn, and README all form a consistent professional identity. After this module I completely revamped how I present myself online.

Key Takeaway Every GitHub commit and LinkedIn post contributes to my professional brand.
04

Continuous Learning & Upskilling

PPW emphasized that graduation is not the end of learning — it is the beginning. Technologies change rapidly; a developer who stops learning risks becoming irrelevant. This insight motivated me to pursue structured online learning alongside my degree, dedicating 4–5 hours weekly to platforms like Coursera, LinkedIn Learning, and Simplilearn, focusing on cloud computing and DevOps.

Key Takeaway Learning how to learn effectively is the most transferable skill a developer can have.
05

Teamwork & Collaboration

Through projects like the LMS, Pet Adoption Portal, and ElectroMart on GitHub, I had collaboration experience — but PPW gave me a theoretical framework via Belbin Team Roles, meeting management, and conflict resolution. I connected these concepts to our GitHub workflows: pull requests, code reviews, and issue tracking are professional collaboration practices, not just technical ones.

Key Takeaway PPW turned me from someone who participates in teams to someone who actively builds high-performing teams.
